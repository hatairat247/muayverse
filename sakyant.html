Sak Yant: The Spiritual
Tattoos of Muay Thai

Thailand, deeply rooted in Buddhist traditions, views "Sak Yant" (Yantra Tattooing) not merely as body
art, but as a sacred ritual intertwining religion, magic, and culture. Muay Thai fighters and martial artists
worldwide seek these tattoos to invoke blessings of power, protection, and fortune,
as well as to symbolize respect for their teachers and the art itself.

The term "Sak Yant" translates to "Magic Tattoo" or "Sacred Yantra." Authentically, these designs must
be bestowed by Buddhist monks or spiritual masters (Arjarn) who have undergone rigorous training in the magical arts.

Gao Yord (Nine Peaks)

Typically inked at the base of the neck, the Gao Yord is regarded as the "Yant Kru" (Master Yant). It offers supreme protection against weapons and danger while bestowing "Metta Mahaniyom" (Great Popularity and Loving Kindness) upon the wearer.

Hah Taew (Five Lines)

A renowned design for enhancing destiny, career success, and warding off negativity. Each of the five sacred lines carries a specific blessing:
Row 1: Protects against unjust punishment, cleanses unwanted spirits, and safeguards the home.
Row 2: Reverses bad horoscopes and protects against bad luck.
Row 3: Shields against black magic, curses, and malevolent intent.
Row 4: Enhances good fortune, success, and ambition.
Row 5: Boosts charm (Metta) and attraction to the opposite sex, while amplifying the fourth row.

Hanuman (The Monkey God)

Hanuman symbolizes invincibility, agility, and immense bravery. It is believed that this Yant grants the wearer the Monkey God's strength and the ability to evade danger effortlessly. Many boxers believe it provides the mental fortitude and courage required to conquer opponents in the ring.

Suea Ku (Twin Tigers)

One of the most popular designs for fighters, the Twin Tigers represent absolute strength, power, and authority (Barami). Usually tattooed on the mid-back, this Yant is believed to offer protection and instill fear and hesitation in opponents.

Paed Tidt (Eight Directions)

This sacred Yant represents protection from all eight compass directions. It is believed to offer universal safeguarding, ensuring the wearer is shielded from harm no matter where they travel or which direction danger may come from.
